Guidelines:

Want to write for healthHunt? Jump aboard!
By writing for us, you can make a difference, touch lives, reach out to a huge base, and expand your repertoire and skills – all with no pressure!

  • We believe a healthy lifestyle should be top priority for everyone. Our focus is health – physical, mental, spiritual, emotional, social, and sexual.
  • Write to us with inspiring stories (yours or someone else’s), how-to pieces (recipes, yoga poses, anxiety relievers…whatever floats your boat) etc.
  • Talk to us about the benefits of avocado or decoding aura reading…it turns us on.
  • If you think there are topics we need to talk about that we haven’t yet, feel free to send in suggestions – in the form of an article.
  • We are young, and we write for the youth. Set the tone with your sass, but don’t be classist, racist, sexist, or any other kind of –ist.
  • Ultimately, your goal should be to motivate and inspire people to live and think positively.
  • Please note that the person who submits the story will be the author of the article.

Dos and Don’ts:

  • We advocate moderation. Your articles should reflect the same.
  • The articles should be in UK English.
  • Send us at least 1 picture with each article (see guidelines for photo submissions).
  • Word count should be between 600 and 1000 words.
  • Let your tips be bite-sized and actionable. Let people know that living healthy does not equal difficult.
  • Be specific. Eg: ‘Replace butter with avocado in your sandwich’, instead of ‘eat healthy’.
  • Send complete articles. Include a title, citations, and sources from where you have taken the information, your headshot, a short bio (around 120 words), and links to your website and/or social media pages.
  • Don’t be a copycat. Send submissions on original topics, and in your words. Plagiarism will not be tolerated. There will be legal consequences to you/healthhunt if you plagiarise, so when in doubt, always cite.
  • Make sure your work has been fact checked.
  • Run your article through www.quetext.com before you send your submission.
  • We don’t like judgement. Neither in tone, nor in information. Please keep that in mind.
  • Don’t plug products or services, please.
  • Send completed articles to submit@healthhunt.in

Specifics:

  • Workout Posts – Whether it is a yoga pose, or an article for building great biceps, write detailed instructions. Each activity should have a photo. We will be happy to include a (short) video if you have one.
  • Recipes – Start by writing a few lines about the dish (why you like it, the health benefits of it etc.), give exact measurements for the ingredients, and detailed instructions for preparation. Add an original photograph.
  • Mental Wellbeing – When writing on this, don’t assume the reader is familiar with depression, meditation, or any other aspect of mental wellbeing that you might be writing about.
  • Success Stories (or other personal articles) – Start from the beginning, include your feelings at each step, show your motivation, and tell us the end result.

At the end of it all, if you are still not sure about certain elements, check out our previous posts, or write to us at submit@healthhunt.in

Photo and Video Guidelines:

  • All submissions need to have at least 1 photograph, of at least 450 x 300.
  • All images need to be of a good quality, and labeled properly.
  • We strive to maintain consistency, and to that end, please see the following:
  • Try and use the landscape format.
  • Nothing better than natural light for photographs!
  • In case of photos for recipes, try to use neutral backgrounds, surfaces, tableware, and cutlery.
  • In case of workout poses, the best shots come out in an outside environment (example: a park).
  • The photograph should not be under or over-exposed.
  • Look at the image before you submit it. Are you happy with it? Is it too one-dimensional? Does it have enough colour? Does it have too much colour? Is it pleasing to the eye?
  • If submitting a video, make sure to edit all extraneous and extra footage.
  • Videos should be well-lit, and well-shot.
